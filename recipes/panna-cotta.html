Buttermilk Panna Cotta

This takes the traditional panna cotta a step forward and gives it a nice tangy flavor like a good greek yogurt. It excellently pairs with sweet fruit compotes or purees. I liked to serve it with a nice cookie for some texture.

For this recipe you will need:

  • Liquid measuring cup
  • Measuring spoons
  • Non-reactive pot
  • Whisk
  • Digital thermometer
  • 6 x 8-10oz Ramikens

Ingredients:

3.5oz Sugar
Pinch Salt
2 tsp Powdered gelatin
2 Cups Cream
2 Cups Buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. In the pot whisk together the sugar, salt, and gelatin
  2. Whisk in the cream and allow the gelatin to bloom for 5 minutes.
  3. Heat the cream mixture on medium low until it reaches 150°F - 160°F, whisking occasionally.
  4. Removed from heat and allow the mixture to cool down to 105°F - 110°F.
  5. Add the cold buttermilk and divide between the ramikens.
  6. Place ramikens uncovered in the fridge to set, roughly 6 hours.

Note: For chocolate panna cotta add 3oz of fine chopped dark chocolate to the hot mixture once it is removed from the heat.
