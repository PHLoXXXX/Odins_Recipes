Foccacia

This recipe is very forgiving. If you are off on your timing it will still be delicious. It also doesnt take much hands on work to make.

For this recipe you will need:

  • Kitchen scale
  • large sheet tray or cassarole dish
  • dough scraper
  • stand mixer (optional)

Recipe

% Qty. Ingredient
100 1351g Flour, Bread
95 1284g Water
2 27g Salt
1.5 20g Yeast
2.5 34g Sugar or Honey
8 108g Olive Oil (Plus extra for baking)

Instructions

  1. Combine flour, sugar, yeast, salt in a large bowl and mix thuroughly.
  2. Pour the water into the bowl and use your dough scraber to mix the dough until there is no dry flour.
  3. While spinning the bowl and scrapping around the outside mix the dough stretching it with every scrape to build a bit of structure in the dough
  4. After mixing for about 1 - 2 minutes pour the oil around the sides of the bowl then lift and stretch the sides into the middle.
  5. Cover and let the dough bulk ferment until doubled in size, giving it one fold half way through, roughly 2 hours total.
  6. Oil your pan or dish then fold your dough one more time and tranfer to the oppiled dish.
  7. Cover again and allow to rise for 45 - 60 minutes.
  8. Preheat oven to 450°F.
  9. Uncover, drizzle heavily with oil, sprinkle with large flake sea salt then with oiled hands, poke the dough down with your fingertips creating deep dimples all over the surface.
  10. Once oven is preheated place the foccacia in the center of the oven and bake 20 - 30 minutes, or until the center temps 180 - 190°F.
  11. Allow the foccacia to cool completely, cut and enjoy!
